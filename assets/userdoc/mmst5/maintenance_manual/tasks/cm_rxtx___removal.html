RxTx - Removal

Removal of the RxTx.

  1. Create a transceiver backup file with the Radar Service Application. For this use the Backup/Restore view.
  2. Stop antenna rotation (if active) with the Radar Service Application.
  3. Switch “Mains Off” with the Radar Service Application.
  4. Turn off power to the transceiver with the power switch on the Power Supply Unit
  5. Loosen X6 on the Waveguide Assy and X14 on the RxTx with Torque wrench, 1 Nm and Extender for torque wrench (see Figure 1).
    Figure 1. Connectors 1
  6. Remove the small cable, that connects X6 with X14 (see Figure 1).
  7. Mount the Termination on X14, which is attached to the RxTx with a chain (see Figure 2).
    Figure 2. Termination
  8. On RxTx, loosen all SMA Connectors (X1 - X14) with Torque wrench, 1 Nm and Extender for torque wrench and two D-Sub Connectors (X15 - X16) with Screwdriver, 3,5 mm x 100 (see red circles in Figure 3).
    Figure 3. Connectors 2
  9. On the RxTx Controller, loosen and remove the SMA Connectors X1 and X10 and the two D-Sub Connectors X12 and X14 as these might obstruct free passage for the RxTx when pulled out (see Figure 3).
  10. On the RxTx Controller, turn the semi-rigid cables from the SMA Connectors X2 - X9, so the RxTx has free passage when it is taken out (see yellow circles in Figure 3).
  11. On the Waveguide Assy, loosen X2 (only slightly), so the cable can be turned away from the RxTx.
  12. With the Screwdriver PH2 x 300 loosen and remove the four screws, which fix the RxTx to the back (see red circles in Figure 4).
    The two lower screws are accessible through the cable tray at the bottom - the two upper screws are accessible through the two slots on the front plate (see red circles in Figure 5).
    Figure 4. Screws
    Figure 5. Two Slots
  13. Take out the RxTx module and place it in an anti-static bag.
